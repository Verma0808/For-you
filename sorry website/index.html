My dearest Anjali,

I keep replaying that moment, and every time, I like myself a little less in it. I let my frustration turn into anger, and I let that anger land on you — the one person who never deserved to catch it. I'm sorry. Not the quick kind of sorry, the kind I actually mean.

You didn't do anything to earn the way I spoke to you. There's no context that makes it okay, and I'm not going to look for one. I was wrong, plain and simple, and I own that completely.

What hurts most is knowing I made you feel small, or unsafe, or unheard — when the whole point of us is that you should feel exactly the opposite with me.

I don't want you to just hear "I'm sorry" and move on. I want you to know I'm actually going to work on this — on my temper, on pausing before I speak, on giving you the patience and gentleness you give me without even trying.

You mean more to me than my pride, more than being "right" in any argument ever could.

Please forgive me for everything that happened between us. I'm truly sorry for getting angry with you and for the way I behaved — you didn't deserve that, and I genuinely regret my actions. I never wanted to hurt you or make you feel bad.

I know I made a mistake, and I'm sincerely sorry from the bottom of my heart. I hope you can forgive me and give me a chance to make things right.

Thank you for putting up with me even when I make it hard. I love you, Anjali — quietly, loudly, and every way in between. I'm sorry, and I'm here, however long it takes.

Yours, always
